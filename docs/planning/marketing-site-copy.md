### FAQ (accordion — collapsed by default, expands on tap)

**Trust and risk**

**Is my payment information secure?**
Yes. We never see or store your card details.

**What happens if something goes wrong during the draft?**
Email support@draftontheclock.com. We actively monitor it and respond fast. That's for app issues — if your running back gets hurt in Week 3, that one's on us to feel bad about with you, not fix.

**Is this a new platform? Who's behind it?**
Yes, brand new. Built by a longtime fantasy football commissioner who got tired of running draft night by hand and decided to fix it. Read the full story on our About page.

**Tell us how it went**
We're new, and we want to hear about it, good or bad. Reach out anytime at support@draftontheclock.com.

**Compatibility and fit**

**Does this replace my ESPN, Yahoo, or Sleeper league?**
No. On The Clock runs your live draft. Once the draft's done, you're back on whatever platform you already use for the season.

**Do my managers need to create accounts?**
Yes, but not until you're ready. Set up your league first, then send your managers the invite link. Each one creates a free account in under a minute.

**Does this support snake or auction drafts?**
Snake draft only.

**Technical and device**

**Do I need a laptop or desktop?**
For the commissioner, yes. Setup, league creation, and running the draft are all built for desktop, and that's where they work best. Managers can draft comfortably from a phone. Everyone gets a better experience on desktop, commissioner and manager both, but for managers, mobile is genuinely fine.

**What if my internet drops mid-draft?**
Your picks are saved the second they happen, not backed up later. Reconnect and you're right back in, no lost progress, no refresh needed.

**How many people can join a draft?**
Built for standard league sizes, up to 14 teams.

**Product mechanics**

**Can I customize scoring and league settings?**
Yes. Fully custom scoring and roster settings, set by the commissioner before the draft.

**What happens to the results afterward?**
Full results, exportable as PDF or CSV, right after the draft ends.

**Purchase**

**Is this a one-time payment or a subscription?**
One-time. $49.95 per league, per draft.

**What's your refund policy?**
All sales are final.

## ABOUT PAGE

**Headline:** Why this exists

**Body:**

Twenty years ago, our league drafted on Yahoo's built-in tool over dial-up. Connections dropped. People got auto-picked. Not everyone could even get into the room.

So we stopped drafting online and started drafting in person. Food, drinks, trash talk, one night a year the whole league showed up for.

Somebody still had to run it. That was the commissioner. Every year, for twenty years, that was the same person: set up the room, sort out the screen and the connection, figure out how to decide draft order (a hat, beer pong, a Wonderlic test, a backyard Olympics, whatever it was that year), settle the league's rule debates, and track every vote by hand.

Then the draft itself. Running the clock off a phone. Typing in every pick. Calling out the ten-second warning. Memorizing the board well enough to catch it when someone tried to draft a player already taken three rounds back.

Everyone else got to focus on their team. The commissioner didn't.

We got used to it. Then we didn't want to be used to it anymore.

On The Clock is what we built instead: a live draft platform that runs the parts of draft night nobody enjoys running, so the commissioner gets to draft too.

A draft tool for commissioners. Built by one.

I opened About with the dial-up scene instead of "twenty years commissioning," since the hero already owns that line — About earns its place by adding the specific sequence of facts the hero doesn't have room for, not by restating the headline in paragraph form.

I kept "we" instead of "I" throughout About. Worth a decision: the rest of the site speaks as a company (On The Clock, Strategic Legacy Collective), and About is the one place a first-person "I built this because..." might land harder and feel more accountable than "we." Tell me if you want that switched.

I did not use the word "sentimental" anywhere and tried to keep every sentence a fact rather than a feeling, per your note. If any line still reads as reaching for emotion rather than stating what happened, flag the specific line and I'll flatten it further.
